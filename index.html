I am a Ph.D. candidate at the University of Southern California (USC)’s Department of Political Science and International Relations. I study the international political economy and gender politics of East Asia.

The dissertation project examines the role of conscription and economic uncertainty on political polarization of young voters in East Asian democracies. My other research areas include the sociopolitical causes and consequences of low fertility rates and how sectoral dynamics affected East Asian development and continue to influence economic growth in the region today. My research has been supported by the Korean Studies Institute, the Willard and Betty Beling Scholarship and the Sherwin Family Scholarship at USC.

My work leverages a wide range of research designs and data sources, including novel survey experiments, interviews and process tracing, as well as causal inference methods for large-n panel data. I am also fluent in Mandarin Chinese and Korean and have extensive fieldwork experience in South Korea.

In my free time, I love to go on hikes with my partner, support my hometown hockey team, the San Jose Sharks, and hunt for new restaurants and recipes to try!
